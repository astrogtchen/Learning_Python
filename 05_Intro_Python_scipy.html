Planck Function¶

T

$$ B_{\nu}(\nu, T) = \frac{2h \nu^3}{c^2} \frac{1}{e^{\frac{h\nu}{kT}}-1} $$

$$ B_{\lambda}(\lambda, T) = \frac{2hc^2}{\lambda^5} \frac{1}{e^{\frac{hc}{\lambda kT}}-1}$$

h = Planck constantk = Boltzmann constantc = speed of light in vaccum

The SI units of $B_{\nu}$ are $ Wsr^{1}m^{2}Hz^{1}$, while those of $B_{\lambda}$ are $Wsr^{1}m^{3}$

spectral energy density (u) $$ u_{\nu}(\nu, T) = \frac{4\pi}{c} B_{\nu}(\nu, T) = \frac{8\pi h \nu^3}{c^3} \frac{1}{e^{\frac{h\nu}{kT}}-1} $$

$$ u_{\lambda}(\lambda, T) = \frac{4\pi}{c} B_{\lambda}(\lambda, T) = \frac{8\pi hc}{\lambda^5} \frac{1}{e^{\frac{hc}{\lambda kT}}-1}$$

Energy density¶

$$\frac{U}{V} = \int_{0}^{\infty} u_{\nu}(\nu, T) d\nu = \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{8\pi h \nu^3}{c^3} \frac{1}{e^{\frac{h\nu}{kT}}-1} d\nu = \frac{4\sigma T^4}{c}$$

$ x = \frac{h\nu}{kT} $ $dx = \frac{h}{kT} d\nu$ $$\frac{U}{V} = \frac{8\pi (kT)^4}{(hc)^3} \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{x^3}{e^{x}-1} dx $$

Bose-Einstein integral, Riemann zeta function $ \zeta (4)$ Gamma function $$ J = \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{x^3}{e^{x}-1} dx = \zeta(4) \Gamma(4) =\frac{\pi^4}{90} \times 6 =\frac{\pi^4}{15} $$

Riemann zeta function$: \zeta(s) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^{s}}$

Gamma function: $ \Gamma(n) = \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{x^{n-1}}{e^{x}} dx = (n-1) !$

$$\frac{U}{V} = \frac{8 \pi^5 (kT)^4}{15(hc)^3} =aT^4 $$ $a$ radiation constant

$$ a = 7.56 \times 10^{-16} Jm^{-3}K^{-4}$$

計算過程¶

ref :23220014$\zeta$

$$ \frac{1}{e^{x}-1} = \frac{1}{e^{x} \left( 1-e^{-x} \right) } = e^{-x} \left( 1+e^{-x}+e^{-2x}+ \cdots \right) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} e^{-nx}$$

$$ J = \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{x^3}{e^{x}-1} dx = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \int_{0}^{\infty}e^{-nx} x^{3} dx$$

adopt integration by part

$$ \int_{0}^{\infty}e^{-nx} x^{3} dx = \frac{3}{n}\int_{0}^{\infty}e^{-nx} x^{2} dx = \frac{3 \cdot 2}{n^2}\int_{0}^{\infty}e^{-nx} x dx =\frac{3 \cdot 2 \cdot 1}{n^3}\int_{0}^{\infty}e^{-nx} dx = \frac{3!}{n^{3}} \times \frac{1}{n} = \frac{3!}{n^4}$$

$$ \therefore J=3! \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^4} = 3! \zeta \left( 4 \right)$$

$\zeta$ $$\zeta(s) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^{s}} $$

$s>1$$s$

$$ x^{2} = \frac{\pi^2}{3} + 4 \sum_{1}^{\infty} (-1)^{n} \frac{\cos nx}{n^2}$$

$$ x^{4} = \frac{\pi^4}{5} + \sum_{1}^{\infty} (-1)^{n} \left( \frac{8\pi^2}{n^2} - \frac{48}{n^4} \right) \cos nx$$

$x = \pi $ $$ \pi^2 = \frac{\pi^2}{3} + 4 \sum_{1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} \Rightarrow \zeta(2)= \sum_{1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2}= \frac{\pi^2}{6}$$

$$ \frac{4}{5} \pi^4 = 8 \pi^2 \zeta(2) - 48 \zeta(4) \Rightarrow \zeta(4) = \frac{\pi^4}{90}$$

$$J = 3! \zeta \left( 4 \right) = \frac{\pi^4}{15}$$

Parseval's identity:

f(x) $$ f(x) = \frac{a_{0}}{2} + \sum_{1}^{\infty}a_{n}\cos nx + \sum_{1}^{\infty}b_{n} \sin nx (-\pi<x<\pi)$$

Parseval's identity $$ \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} \lbrack f(x) \rbrack^{2} dx = \frac{{a_{0}}^{2}}{2} + \sum_{1}^{\infty} \left( {a_{n}}^2 + {b_{n}}^2 \right)$$

$f(x)=x^2$ $a_{0}= \frac{2}{3}\pi^2$$a_{n}=\frac{4(-1)^n}{n^2}$

$$ \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} \lbrack x^2 \rbrack^{2} dx = \frac{(\frac{2}{3}\pi^2)^2}{2} + \sum_{1}^{\infty} (-1)^{2n}\frac{16}{n^4}$$

$$\frac{1}{\pi} \frac{x^5}{5} \bracevert_{-\pi}^{\pi}= \frac{2}{5}\pi^4 = \frac{2}{9}\pi^4 + \sum_{1}^{\infty} \frac{16}{n^4} \Rightarrow \zeta(4) = \frac{\pi^4}{90} $$

Stefan-Boltzmann law:¶

The StefanBoltzmann law gives the power emitted per unit area of the emitting body,

$$\frac{P}{A} = \int_{0}^{\infty} B_{\nu}(\nu, T)d\nu \int d\Omega$$

Lambert's cosine law: the intensity observed along the sphere will be the actual intensity times the cosine of the zenith angle ,

$$\frac{P}{A} =\int_{0}^{\infty} B_{\nu}(\nu, T)d\nu \int_{0}^{2\pi} d\theta \int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}} \cos \phi \sin \phi d\phi = \pi \int_{0}^{\infty} B_{\nu}(\nu, T)d\nu = \frac{c}{4} \frac{U}{V} = \sigma T^{4} $$

$$\sigma = \frac{2\pi^5 k^4}{15c^2 h ^3} = \frac{ac}{4} = 5.67\times 10^{-8} Js^{-1}m^{-2}K^{-4}$$

單位體積的光子數量¶

$$ n = \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{u_{\nu}(\nu, T)}{h\nu} d\nu = \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{8\pi \nu^2}{c^3} \frac{1}{e^{\frac{h\nu}{kT}}-1} d\nu $$

$ x = \frac{h\nu}{kT} $ $dx = \frac{h}{kT} d\nu$ $$\frac{N}{V} = \frac{8\pi (kT)^3}{(hc)^3} \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{x^2}{e^{x}-1} dx $$

$$ N = \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{x^2}{e^{x}-1} dx = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \int_{0}^{\infty}e^{-nx} x^{2} dx $$

$$ \int_{0}^{\infty}e^{-nx} x^{2} dx = \frac{2}{n}\int_{0}^{\infty}e^{-nx} x dx = \frac{2 \cdot 1}{n^2}\int_{0}^{\infty}e^{-nx} dx = \frac{2!}{n^{2}} \times \frac{1}{n} = \frac{2!}{n^3}$$

$$N = 2 \times \zeta(3)$$

$\zeta(3)\approx 1.202 $$N \approx 2.404$

$$ n = \frac{8\pi (kT)^3}{(hc)^3} \times 2.404 \approx 20.2 T^3 photons/cm^3 $$

$T=2.7$K $$n(T=2.7K) \approx 400 photons/cm^3$$

SHM¶

$$ m \ddot{x} + b \dot{x} + kx = A \sin(x) $$

$$ \Rightarrow \ddot{x} + \frac{b}{m} \dot{x} + \frac{k}{m}x = \frac{A}{m} \sin(x)$$

define $\omega_0 = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}\omega_{b}=\sqrt{\frac{b}{2m}}a = \frac{A}{m}$

$$ \ddot{x} + 2\omega_{b}^{2}\dot{x} +\omega_{0}^{2}x = a \sin(x)$$

let $ u=\dot{x}$, then

$$ \dot{u} + 2\omega_{b}^{2} u +\omega_{0}^{2}x = a \sin(x)$$

SHM:¶

$$\vec{a} = -\omega^{2} \vec{x}$$

$\Delta t$ SHM$x_{i-1}x_{i}x_{i+1}$ $$a = \lim_{\Delta t \rightarrow 0} \frac{v_{k+1}-v_{k}}{\Delta t}$$

$$v_{k}=\lim_{\Delta t \rightarrow 0} \frac{x_{i}-x_{i-1}}{\Delta t}v_{k+1}=\lim_{\Delta t \rightarrow 0} \frac{x_{i+1}-x_{i}}{\Delta t}$$

$$a = \lim_{\Delta t \rightarrow 0} \frac{ \frac{x_{i+1}-x_{i}}{\Delta t}- \frac{x_{i}-x_{i-1}}{\Delta t}}{\Delta t}$$

$$\Rightarrow a = (x_{i+1}-2x_{i}+x_{i-1}) \Delta t^{-2} =\omega^{2}x_{i}$$

$$\Rightarrow (x_{i+1}-2x_{i}+x_{i-1}) =- \omega^{2}\Delta t ^{2}x_{i}= - (\frac{2\pi \Delta t }{T})^{2}x_{i} $$

$T =2 \pi$ $\Delta t = 0.1 $

$$ (x_{i+1}-2x_{i}+x_{i-1}) =- 0.01 x_{i} \Rightarrow x_{i+1}=1.99x_{i}-x_{i-1}$$

from scipy.optimize import curve_fit

Use non-linear least squares to fit a function, f, to data.

Assumes ydata = f(xdata, *params) + eps

scipy.optimize.curve_fit(fn, xdata, ydata, p0=())

fn : model function p0 : Initial guess for the parameters. If None, then the initial values will all be 1

return:

  • popt : array

Optimal values for the parameters so that the sum of the squared residuals of f(xdata, *popt) - ydata is minimized

  • pcov : 2d array

The estimated covariance of popt. The diagonals provide the variance of the parameter estimate. To compute one standard deviation errors on the parameters use perr = np.sqrt(np.diag(pcov)).
